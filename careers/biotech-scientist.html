Women Who Lead the Way

Jennifer Doudna
Biochemist & Biotechnology Pioneer; Professor, UC Berkeley; Co-inventor of CRISPR-Cas9


Photo Credit: Wikimedia Commons

“With the power of CRISPR comes a responsibility to use it wisely.”

Jennifer Doudna co-developed CRISPR-Cas9, a revolutionary gene-editing technology that transformed biotechnology and medicine by enabling precise edits to DNA. Her work has accelerated advances in treating genetic diseases, developing new therapies, and expanding how scientists understand and engineer life itself.

Mini-Activity: Try This!

“Think Like a Biotech Scientist” Challenge

Scenario: A new virus is spreading quickly. Scientists need a faster way to detect it.

Your Mission:

  1. What sample would you test? (Blood, saliva, cells, etc.)
  2. What data would you collect?
  3. How could technology (AI, sensors, or automation) help speed up results?

Write or sketch your idea—there’s no single right answer!
